\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{The Acceptance Criterion Is Not Met on Held-Out Data}
\label{subsec:disc-finding}

On the 38-report holdout, GPT-4o mini reached Cohen's $\kappa = 0.235$ on Raw
reports and $\kappa = 0.099$ on Improved reports. Both values fall well below the
$\kappa \geq 0.70$ criterion we fixed before the holdout run, and both sit in the
lower half of the Landis and Koch scale~\cite{landis1977measurement}: fair
agreement for Raw and only slight agreement for Improved. Read against the
research question, the answer is negative. The model does not agree with human
annotators on steps-to-reproduce executability strongly enough to be treated as a
substitute for manual assessment on this dataset. We state this plainly rather
than soften it, because the size of the gap, not its direction, is what carries
the lesson.

\subsection{Accuracy Conceals the Disagreement}
\label{subsec:disc-accuracy}

The two conditions produced identical accuracy, $0.684$ (26 of 38), yet their
kappa values differ by more than a factor of two. This is exactly the situation
that motivated our choice of a chance-corrected metric~\cite{cohen1960coefficient}.
Because the holdout label distribution is skewed, a predictor can match roughly
two thirds of the labels while contributing little beyond the majority class. The
Improved condition makes the point concrete: the model predicted Executable for
37 of 38 reports and Non-Executable for only one. It never mislabeled a genuinely
executable report, but it also flagged almost none of the deficient ones, leaving
a single true negative out of thirteen Non-Executable cases. In effect, the
evaluator rubber-stamps improved reports as reproducible instead of judging them.
On Raw reports the predictions are less lopsided (12 Executable, 26
Non-Executable), which is why the Raw kappa, though still weak, stays above the
Improved kappa.

\subsection{Calibration Does Not Transfer to Unseen Reports}
\label{subsec:disc-generalization}

The contrast between calibration and holdout is the central result. On the
development subset used to tune the prompt, agreement was substantial to almost
perfect (Raw $\kappa \approx 0.87$, Improved $\kappa \approx 0.81$). None of that
strength survived on the protected holdout. The drop is not a small erosion; it is
a collapse from almost-perfect to slight agreement once the reports were no longer
part of prompt design. Any evaluation that had measured agreement on the same data
used to build the evaluator would have reported a misleadingly strong result. This
is the concrete form of a caution raised in the LLM-as-judge
literature~\cite{li2024judges}: an automated evaluator must be validated on data it
was not shaped against before its agreement figures can be trusted.

\subsection{Relation to Prior Work and Implications}
\label{subsec:disc-implications}

ImproBR reports that its pipeline raises the share of executable steps to
reproduce on Mojira from $28.8\%$ to $67.6\%$~\cite{akyol2026improbr}. Our findings
add a cautionary counterpart from the evaluation side. When the same model family
is asked to judge those improved reports, it defaults to Executable and loses the
ability to separate a genuinely reproducible improved report from one that is still
deficient. An LLM evaluator can therefore echo the optimism of an LLM improver
without adding independent discriminative value, which is precisely the failure
mode that validation against human labels is meant to catch~\cite{li2024judges,hou2024llm4se}.

The practical reading follows directly. On this domain, GPT-4o mini is not reliable
enough to replace human reproducibility triage. On Raw reports it retains a weak but
non-trivial signal and could, under human oversight, serve as a coarse pre-filter
that surfaces likely non-reproducible reports for review. On Improved reports it is
unsuitable as an independent judge, because it approves almost everything. One more
bound is worth keeping in view: the two human annotators themselves agreed only at
$\kappa \approx 0.76$, so executability is an inherently difficult judgment even for
people. The model's holdout agreement, however, falls far short of that human
ceiling rather than approaching it.
